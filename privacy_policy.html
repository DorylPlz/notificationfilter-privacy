Privacy Policy — NotificationFilter

Last updated: 2026-06-24

NotificationFilter ("the app") is an Android application that filters notifications from apps you choose. This policy describes what data the app processes and how it is handled.

Summary

  • All data stays on your device.
  • The app does not sell, share, or upload your data — we do not operate backend servers for this app.
  • Uninstalling the app removes its local database and settings.

Data the app accesses

Notification access (required)

With your explicit permission in Android Settings, the app reads notifications from other apps to decide whether to block them. This may include the app name and package name, notification title and text, and notification channel or category metadata. The app uses this content only to apply your filter rules. Blocked notifications may be saved locally in the in-app history (see below).

Installed applications

The app lists applications that have a launcher icon so you can choose which apps to filter. Package names and app labels are read from the device. Icons are processed locally for display only.

Optional diagnostics (disabled in Play Store builds)

Development builds may write local diagnostic logs to device storage for up to 3 days. Release builds distributed on Google Play have this feature disabled.

Data stored on device

The app stores the following data locally in SQLite:

  • Your filter settings (apps enabled, keywords, channel rules)
  • A history of blocked notifications (title, body, app name, timestamp) retained for 14 days, then deleted automatically
  • Optional reinforced-mode preference

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.
